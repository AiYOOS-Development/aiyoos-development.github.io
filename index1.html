Summarize the primary precedents regarding search engine monopolies in the last decade.

Requested by Partner, 2 mins ago

Based on the analysis of 12,000+ court documents, the legal landscape regarding search engine monopolies has shifted significantly. The primary focus has moved from "consumer welfare" price-based arguments to "exclusionary conduct" preventing competition.

Key Determinations

In US v. Google LLC, the court emphasized that default search agreements act as a barrier to entry. The ruling highlighted that scale is necessary for search quality, creating a feedback loop that cements dominance.

  • Default Agreements: Contracts with browser vendors (Apple, Mozilla) are now scrutinized as exclusionary.
  • Data Scale: Access to user interaction data is considered a critical infrastructure for competition.
